Inventory management

Inventory management is an integral part of any successful business, serving to provide uninterrupted production, sales and/or customer service at a minimum cost.

 

Inventories usually consist of goods, raw materials and finished products. Since each of these elements is essentially equal to money for the business owner, effective management of these assets is key to making a profit and having optimum stock of items in your inventory. An inventory management system tracks the sale, purchase and payments related to these elements of inventory. Ineffective inventory management can contribute to losses or business failure, since this crucial component is so vital to the profits and costs of the business. Well-managed inventory is important because businesses rely heavily on these items to make a profit. Even if you don't need an inventory tracking system now, it may become critical as your business grows. Without a carefully balanced and managed inventory system, your business could suffer from a lack of organization and may lose revenue as a result. (QuickBooks)

Computerized inventory management systems are flexible and the information it contains make reporting easier then manual inventory management. With the reports, you can get a stock valuation; find out how fast of a turn over for an item in stock and the number of items in stock. QuickBooks is a good option for small businesses dealing with different types of items in their inventory.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Inventory In QuickBooks

Incorporating inventory into your QuickBooks takes planning and more of a systematic approach to its implementation.

 

 

Inventory affects all areas of QuickBooks functions, from estimating to invoicing from production to shipping. Inventory changes affects the workflow of the business.

Inventory starts with the Item List, where you will create a new Inventory part to track. Other lists you have to have setup are:

  • the item list
  • the vendor list
  • the Chart of Accounts
  • the Units of Measure


QuickBooks items connects to the financial accounts, saving the non-accountant from the difficulty of accounting. This guide will show you only about setting up inventory items. So, remember to set your lists first start with the accounts, and then connect your items to them before you begin to set up inventory items.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 


Inventory Items

 

The item list contains what you sell or buy all transactions can involve items in the item list. We will deal with the types of items for the inventory below.

 

There are various item types in the item list but let us focus the items types for inventory:

  • Inventory part  Merchandise or parts you purchase, track as inventory, and then resell. (SCOTT)
  • Non-inventory parts  Materials or parts you buy but dont keep on hand as inventory.
    These can be either part of your overhead (like office supplies or work vehicles), or they can be materials you buy to finish a specific job and charge back to your customer. (SCOTT)

 

 

 

 

 

 

Items can also be discounts, sales tax, and subtotals. QuickBooks for Mac has ten different types of items you can use for forms and reports. You can use custom fields let you add details to items you buy and sell, and then use those details to create customized purchase orders, estimates, invoices, and other forms. (SCOTT)

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Creating a new Inventory item

Please Ask Administrator To Enable Inventory Before Creating A New Inventory Item

 

 

        Creating a new Inventory item is easy just make sure you select the correct accounts for your item

From The Home Page Click Items and Services.

   

      1. Select New
    2. Click on Inventory item
    3. In the Purchasing Information section, enter Name, Sub item of, Manufacture Number, and Item Description
    4. In the Purchasing Information section, enter Cost, COGS Account, and the Preferred Vendor
    5. In the Sales Information section enter Sales Price, Tax Code, and the Income Account
    6. In the Inventory Information section enter Asset Account, Reorder Point, and Items On Hand 
    7. Click OK to save the Item
